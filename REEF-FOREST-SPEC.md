## 1. The world: the descent is SOUTH

- **Map**: extend WORLD from 96×64 to **96×128** — the north half is the
  existing Salt Shallows byte-identical (the seed-pinned smoke world and the
  LIVE server world both survive: new rows are appended, nothing moves; the
  `trench-edge` quest anchor sits at today's south rim, which becomes the
  **border scarp**). The south half is the Reef Forest band, generated by its
  own seeded pass (canopy pillars, anemone groves, drowned-town ruins, reef
  resource nodes, a Reefkin settlement site, three chapel ruins for the
  Hourglass).
- **The gate**: the scarp is a broken shelf passable at ONE ramp (landmark:
  "the Stair of Hulls" — a collapsed ship-graveyard ramp). No key, no wall:
  the gate is *practical* — B2 nodes need the Bronze Mattock (shipped,
  inert, activates now via `tool: {tier: 2}` → `gather.tierRequired` on B2
  nodes), B2 enemies hit ~2× B1 (CRAFT spec law), and the twilight starts
  at the scarp (see §2). Crossing it first time fires `set_biomes_cleared(2)`
  (cap 70) + a message that says the number (UI law).
- **Save/live-world migration**: additive — loading a 96×64 save into a
  96×128 world keeps every position valid (north-anchored). One migration
  line in load_game: if the save predates the band, generate the south on
  first load. The shared server keeps its day count and village.
- **Camera limits** update from world size (already derived).

## 2. Light: the biome IS a light-management level

Permanent twilight under the canopy — the day/night tint curve gets a THIRD
band: reef daytime ≈ B1 dusk; reef night is near-black with **angler-lights**
(see creatures). This is where the shipped **Lighthouse-Keeper's Lantern**
(trinket glow + hound-aggro ×0.75) stops being flavor and becomes the best
item in the game — as WORLD-SPEC always promised ("the dark biomes respect
it"). Villagers do NOT settle the reef at EA; expeditions go down, the
village stays in the light. (Their fear of it is already in data:
trait-dark-fearing.)

## 8. The Reefkin & the Brink (the genuinely NEW system)

The one M3 system with no shipped ancestor: `peoples_system.gd` (one system
per file; data/content/peoples/ has both peoples since M0).
- **The settlement**: one Reefkin grove-village in the band — walkable,
  populated by slow gentle half-coral folk with a handful of [E] lines
  (voice: WORLD-SPEC). No trade UI at first contact; their state IS the
  interface.
- **Brink state** (0–100, world-level, saved + synced): readable by walking
  through — population out and working vs. huddled, groves lit vs. dark,
  song vs. silence (audio hook: a chant bed that thins as Brink drops; the
  sfx pipeline handles it). The dying world pushes them down PASSIVELY
  (a slow daily drift once the player has MET them — the WORLD-SPEC law:
  no penalty for peoples you haven't seen; the `met` flag starts the clock).
- **The verbs**: RESTORE (repair reef-pools / ward groves — build-menu
  works at their site, materials real, Brink +) · TRADE (fair barter at a
  post once Brink is stable: pearl/coralwood/reef-iron seed for salt/food —
  the stores economy's first external market) · EXPLOIT (strip a grove /
  drain a pool: windfall + Brink −− + the `peoples` ledger writes) ·
  **NEGLECT** (do nothing after meeting them: the passive drift, counted —
  doing nothing is a choice once you've seen).
- **Brink floor/ceiling**: SAVED (≥80: a Reefkin Crafter joins your village
  — the class exists in npc-classes.json since M0; their trade post opens
  fully) · BRINK (≤20: the groves go dark, the settlement empties, the
  strip-mining is free and the `peoples` ledger remembers forever; their
  dead ARE the resource — the razor stays as WORLD-SPEC wrote it).
- Ledger: the 4th Verdict ledger (`peoples`) exists in verdict tuning since
  M0 — this system finally writes it in volume.
